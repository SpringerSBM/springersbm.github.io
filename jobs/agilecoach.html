The Springer Development Team

Agile Coach

Full time, Springer-Verlag London Ltd

Job duties

  • Strengthen the presence of our team as an Agile centre of excellence by keeping the rest of the organisation aware of our activities.
  • Actively contribute to the organisation’s Agile and Lean community.

Job requirements

Essential:

  • Strong knowledge of, and passion for, Agile principles and supporting practices.
  • Proven experience working with Agile project teams.
  • Understanding of the differences between Agile methods such as Extreme Programming (XP), Scrum and Lean, and how they can be applied together for success.
  • Can explain the costs and benefits of Agile practices such as automated testing, test-driven, development (TDD), continuous delivery and user centred design.
  • Proven experience coaching, teaching and guiding people in the various roles within a project driven organisation.
  • Strong facilitation skills- may have led inceptions, workshops, reviews and retrospectives.
  • Excellent written and oral communication and presentation skills- you will be speaking to senior management as well as delivery teams.
  • Willing to get your hands dirty, i.e. play the role of a Project Manager or BA when necessary.
  • Can take responsibility for managing dependencies between teams and third parties or between teams.
  • Comfortable creating training materials and training programs.
  • Self starting, proactive and enthusiastic, you like to collaborate with others.
  • Prior background in training, consulting or process coaching is a plus.
  • Willing to travel.
  • Knowledge of the software development life cycle.


Please send your CV and covering letter, stating salary expectations and quoting the job reference (SL/PM) to: HR, Springer Science and Business Media Ltd, Floor 6, 236 Gray’s Inn Road, London, WC1X 8HB, or by email to: ukjobs@springer.com.
